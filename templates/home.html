What is IUT RS?

Our activities encompass a wide spectrum, ranging from UAV projects and Mars rover challenges to pioneering initiatives that push the boundaries of technology. Founded on a commitment to excellence and fueled by a passion for robotics, the IUT RS community actively engages in projects that not only reflect our dedication to technological advancement but also contribute to the broader landscape of robotics and automation.

PROJECT AVIJATRIK

IUT Avijatrik, one of the parent teams of Project Altair, was a prominent Mars Rover team in Bangladesh, comprising undergraduate students from the Islamic University of Technology..

Formed in 2018, Team Avijatrik swiftly gained international acclaim in Mars Rover Challenges, collaborating on a rover designed for Martian terrain. Achieving notable success, they secured positions such as 14th global and 2nd in Asia in 2018, 5th globally in 2018, 15th global and 1st in Asia in 2019, and 18th global in 2022 European Rover Challenge, highlighting their consistent excellence.

PROJECT ALTAIR

Project Altair represents a dynamic undertaking led by the IUT Robotic Society, focusing on the creation, building, and functioning of Mars rovers to engage in rover competitions at home and abroad.

Founded in 2023, Project Altair combines IUT's Anirban and Avijatrik rover teams with a focus on shared objectives. In 2023, the team achieved global recognition, securing 10th place in the International Rover Design Challenge and an impressive 10th position in the on-site qualifiers of the 2023 European Rover Challenge, ranking 2nd in Asia and 1st in Bangladesh, highlighting exceptional performance.

PROJECT ANIRBAN

IUT Mars Rover - Anirban, founded in 2015, pioneered Martian exploration with a passionate team. Achieving global recognition, they secured top positions in international competitions, including 16th globally and 2nd in Asia in the 2015 European Rover Challenge. Notably, in 2020, they were 2nd Runners up in the International Rover Challenge. Their consistent excellence continued with a 19th position in the 2021 European Rover Challenge and an impressive 10th position in the 2022 European Rover Challenge (Remote Edition). Team Anirban's accomplishments highlight their competitive prowess on the global stage.

PROJECT AERO

Project Aero, the newest undertaking led by the IUT Robotics Society, is a UAV project. The goal of this project is to design aerial vehicles, such as planes and copters, using UAV technology. Participation in prestigious international tournaments like ISDC and NXP HoverGames is the project's goal.

Drones employ Lidar for precise mapping, RTK GPS for accurate positioning, ELRS for long-range communication, and WiFi Broadcast for efficient data transfer. Initiated in 2019, the project signifies a significant advancement in transportation technology.
